How can use physics to address the limitations of digital computing? This basic question motivated Richard Feynman, John Hopfield, and Carver Mead to propose a new course on “physical computing” back in 1982. This course is at the root of quantum, neuromorphic, and Ising machine engineering. This workshop will examine the current state of those disciplines, highlighting the key challenges and opportunities of designing machines that combine physical and algorithmic principles.
